What I Do

Since entering WashU in the fal of 2014, I have dedicated my time toward exploring different artistic media. Though trained in the fine arts with a particular focus in photography, I have always been interested in more digital media. Communication design, itself, emcompasses a wide range of skills ranging from physical image-making to typesetting to photo editing. The following work is a sampling of the projects I have completed in the past two years in the Communication Design Program. Each project has been one step in my process toward getting a better understanding of whether I would like to take a more illustrative or type-oriented career path.

Alphabet Project

For this assignment, we were asked to create three images that illustrated three letters of our choosing. To do so, we had to pick a subject or character that began with that letter and illustrate it performing an action that began with the same letter.

Self-Portrait 2013

During the first semester of AP Drawing, we were asked to create a variety of pieces that demonstrated the breadth of our skills. This was one of the earlier drawings that I completed during this time, specifically in fulfillment of a general assignment to create a self-portrait. When composing this image, I was interested in playing with diagonals to create a more dynamic composition as well as mark making.

Erasure

For this assignment, we were given a word and asked to incorporate it into an 8 by 16” spread. We were then asked to treat the gutter as a point of transition, with the content on either side illustrating our given word. When creating this piece, I spent the first portion of my process trying to better understand the word “erasure”; did the act of erasing always have to result in a blank canvas? Or is it more equivalent to the act of obliterating something that already exists? In order to play with this concept, I experimented with the use of layers on the two-dimensional paper surface. By layering ink onto the canvas and then using various tools to remove it, I ultimately hoped to see what the remnants of an erasure were.

The Kiss

This drawing was the result of a fairly straightforward exercise meant to familiarize us with pastels. For it, we were asked to choose a relatively famous painting, recompose it, and then recreate it.

Letter Study

This was the first part of an assignment given during the first week of my Typography elective in the fall of 2015. For it, we were asked to choose a letter and create six compositions with it using only black and white. More importantly, the compositions needed to remove the letterforms from the context of being a letter and focus on how it functioned as an autonomous form.

Untitled

Our first assignment in 3D design was to create a 6-foot tall sculpture exclusively using paper and tape. In order to experiment their ranges of behavior, we chose to create a number of variously-sized spheres, each exploring a different variable from texture to density to color.

Self-Portrait, 2015

This was the second part of an assignment that aimed to familiarize us with the use of pastels. Before beginning this drawing, we were asked to choose a well-known, painted self-portrait, and recreate it using only blue and orange pastels. For my drawing, I chose to work with Chuck Close’s Susan painted in 1971. After completing that part of the assignment, we were asked to create a self-portrait in the same style as the first portrait we drew. This time, we were not limited in what colored pastels we could use.

Turtle Sculpture

Our final project in 3D design was to recreate an object (chosen from a collection of fossils and other small, natural objects) at a larger sacle. To do so, we were asked to carve the basic form out of layered insulation foam, which we later covered in spackle in order to add more refined details. For the last step, I chose to apply pastel dust to the spackle using my fingers, in order to slowly build up the patterning of the shell.

Playing Cards

For this assignment, we were asked to create a set of playing cards or tarot cards that were tied together by a theme of our choosing. I ultimately chose to work with the Chinese classical elements, which allowed me to illustrate both the phyical elements and their corresponding heavenly creatures. For the face cards, I chose to create hybrid human-animal creatures, with each element and its astrological associations representing a specific suit.
